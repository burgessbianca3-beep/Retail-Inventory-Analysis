# Retail Inventory Performance Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail inventory and sales performance using SQL and Power BI to uncover trends in revenue, inventory efficiency, promotional effectiveness, and demand forecasting accuracy.

The dashboard was designed to simulate a real-world business intelligence solution used by retail, merchandising, and operations teams to support data-driven decision-making.

The project focuses on answering key business questions such as:

* Which product categories generate the most revenue?
* How do discounts impact units sold?
* Which stores maintain the best inventory efficiency?
* How accurate are demand forecasts?
* Which regions perform best?

# Key Visualizations

## Revenue by Category

Analyzes total revenue generated across product categories to identify top-performing product segments.

## Inventory Efficiency by Store

Compares average inventory levels against average units sold to evaluate operational efficiency across store locations.

## Discount Impact Analysis

Measures how discount levels affect average units sold and identifies the most effective promotional pricing strategy.

## Revenue by Region

Visualizes revenue performance across geographic regions to identify high-performing markets.

## Interactive Filters

Slicers allow users to dynamically filter dashboard results by:

* Region
* Product Category

---

# Key Insights

* Clothing emerged as the top-performing product category based on total revenue.
* A 10% discount level generated the highest average units sold, suggesting an optimal promotional pricing point.
* Revenue performance remained relatively balanced across regions.
* Forecast accuracy reached approximately 94%, indicating strong demand prediction performance.
* Inventory and units sold maintained a positive relationship across stores, supporting efficient inventory allocation.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

* Optimize promotional strategies around the 10% discount level to maximize sales performance.
* Continue investing in top-performing product categories such as Clothing and Furniture.
* Monitor stores with elevated inventory levels to prevent overstocking.
* Use forecasting models to further improve inventory planning and reduce excess stock.
